Material & Construction Analysis

Glass Cooking Chamber

The primary cooking bowl is tempered glass. Glass is chemically stable under normal cooking temperatures and does not require synthetic coatings. Unlike metal baskets with non-stick coatings, glass will never peel, flake, or degrade.

Rack & Internal Components

The rack system is stainless steel. Buyers should confirm whether the specific unit uses 304-grade stainless, which is standard for food applications and provides excellent corrosion resistance.

Heating Element Housing

The upper housing contains electrical components and is not a food-contact surface. As with all countertop appliances, ventilation and proper usage are essential for safe operation and longevity.

Cooking Performance

The convection system provides even heat distribution with minimal hot spots. The 400°F maximum temperature is sufficient for most air frying applications, delivering crispy results comparable to traditional basket-style air fryers.

The transparent bowl allows full visibility during cooking, reducing overcooking and unnecessary reheating. You can monitor food without opening the unit and losing heat.

However, capacity is moderate at 5 quarts. This unit is better suited to individuals or small households rather than large families. The rotisserie function works well for whole chickens up to 4-5 lbs.

The self-cleaning function is a standout feature — simply add water and soap, run the cycle, and rinse. This eliminates the need for harsh scrubbing and helps maintain the glass clarity over time.
