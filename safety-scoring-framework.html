Our Air Fryer Safety Scoring Methodology

Every product we review is evaluated using a structured, material-focused framework. No brand sponsorship influences rankings.

How the 100-Point Score Is Calculated

Each air fryer is evaluated across five weighted categories. The final score reflects food-contact material safety, construction integrity, and long-term durability factors.

  • Food-Contact Materials (30 points)
  • Coating Transparency (25 points)
  • Construction Quality (20 points)
  • Heat Stability & Design (15 points)
  • Manufacturer Transparency (10 points)

Maximum total score: 100 points.

1. Food-Contact Materials (30 Points)

Highest scores are awarded to glass and verified food-grade stainless steel. Lower scores are assigned to coated aluminum or unspecified alloys.

  • 30 pts: Full glass bowl + stainless steel rack
  • 25 pts: Stainless steel basket (verified grade)
  • 15 pts: Mixed metal with partial coating
  • 0–10 pts: Primary PTFE-style coated basket

2. Coating Transparency (25 Points)

We assess whether the manufacturer clearly discloses coating composition and chemical profile.

  • 25 pts: No synthetic non-stick coating used
  • 15 pts: Coating disclosed and certified PFOA-free
  • 5 pts: Limited disclosure
  • 0 pts: No material transparency

3. Construction Quality (20 Points)

  • Weld integrity and fit
  • Thickness of metal components
  • Latch durability
  • Heat insulation design

4. Heat Stability & Design (15 Points)

We consider operating temperature ranges, airflow design, and how materials respond under repeated heat exposure.

5. Manufacturer Transparency (10 Points)

  • Clear material disclosure
  • Accessible customer support
  • Certification documentation
